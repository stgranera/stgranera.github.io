Why I design...

Graphic designers are not only people that know how to push keys
on a keyboard to make things pretty but are problem solvers. As
a designer I find myself taking very complex and abstract ideas and
deconstructing them to their simplest form. I want my work to be
easily understood but not so simple that my works becomes dull
and uninteresting, this is the paradox of being a graphic designer.
Simplicity is beautiful and some times cannot be defined by a language,
but getting to that point can be difficult. Producing work that anyone
from any cultural background can understand is my goal. Every artist
from any field wishes that their work could aspire and break boundaries,
as do I wish for my own work. I have found that the fundamentals of
design play a great deal into the quality of work that one produces.
Without the fundamentals and motivation to solve problems I know my
work can only go so far. I push myself to think abstractly and try to be
inspired by all things. I want my work to be as open and diverse like the
world around me. Loud and bold colors from the pop art era and graffiti
have always demanded my attention. The idea that art work can be
very in your face has intrigued me, and want the fluidity that this kind
of work has to be represented in my work. Keeping in mind the idea of
repetition, organization, transition and reinforcement has helped me take
these abstract and complex problem, and morph them into works of art
that visual communicate with the audience.
